Problem: Popping Ankle

Tightness Release

Step 1: Guasha with a spoon
  1. lubrication with lotion/oil
  2. long slow strokes over the side tissue
  • if you feel a spot like sand paper, target it
  1. strokes over all the soft tissues around ankle
  2. massage over the popping point

Problem: Ankle Instability

Step 1: Plyometrics (Choose 1)
  • Hopping
    • Double (then Single) leg hops in place (3 sets x 30 seconds)
    • Double (then Single) leg hops forward & backward
    • Double (then Single) leg hops side to side
  • Jumping
    • Double leg vertical jump (3 sets x 8 repetitions)
    • Vertical jump 2 to 1
    • Single leg vertical jump (3 sets x 6 repetitions)
    • Single leg forward/lateral/diagonal jump
Step 2: Lower Extremity Strength (Choose 1)
  • Single leg RDL
    • Single leg RDL assist/bodyweight/weighted (3-4 sets x 6-12 repetitions)
  • Lateral step down
    • Lateral step down on increased height (3-4 sets x 6-12 repetitions)
Step 3: Dynamic Balance (Choose 1)
  • Reach with feet (3 sets x 30-60 seconds)
  • Reach with hands
Step 4: Static Balance (Choose 1)
  • single leg stance with eyes open/closed (3 sets x 60 seconds each leg)
  • single leg stance on foam pad with eyes open/closed
  • kettlebell pass on foam pad with eyes open/closed
Step 5: Ankle-Specific Strength
  • Ankle Eversion (3 sets x 25 repetitions)
  • Dorsiflexion (3 sets x 25 repetitions)
  • Heel raise (3-4 sets x 6-12 repetitions)

Problem: The Nasty Knee Cracking

  • quick fix
  • longterm fix: foam roller massage
    • 3-5 minutes warmup

Problem: Jumper’s Knee Rehab

Step 1: Isometrics
  • double/single leg wall sit
  • heel elevated wall sit
  • spanish squat
  • single leg seated knee extension
    • 3-5 sets of 45 sec, 2min rest, 1-3x/day
Step 2: Heavy Slow Resistance
  • tenders are affected more by the rate of loading than the magnitude of the loading, so you can pick any load in this stage as long as it sufficiently loads the patellar tendon
  • squat
  • split squat
  • step down
    • 1-2 exercises | 3-4 sets of 6-15 reps | 2-3 days a week
    • slow tempo: e.g. descend 3 seconds + 1 second at the bottom + ascend 3 seconds
Step 3: Energy Storage & Release
  • combo 1
    • countermovement box jump
    • countermovement jump
    • bilateral depth drop
    • bilateral depth jump
    • single leg depth drop
    • single depth jump
  • combo 2
    • forward lunge
    • forward lunge with step back
    • step and land
    • step and land with step back
    • running with step back
  • hints
    • 2-3 times a week
    • intentionally load the knee extensor mechanism as much as tolerable

Problem: Mobility

Preliminary: Types of Exercises
  • Type 1: Exercises that stretch into that range of motion
    • e.g. improve hip extension range of motion by stretching, options:
      1. static stretching
      2. dynamic stretching
      3. contract & relax
      4. lengthen & strengthen
      5. full range of motion
  • Type 2: Exercises by strengthening the muscles that move your hip in that specific direction
    • e.g. glute bridge and standing cable
Hip Rotation
  • External Rotation
    • stretching hip internal rotators & strengthening hip external rotators
    • seated/supine figure 4 stretch
    • pigeon pose stretching and variants
  • Internal Rotation
    • stretching hip external rotators & strengthening hip internal rotators
    • supine internal rotation stretch
      • 30-60 seconds
  • External & Internal Rotation Together
    • 90/90 Holds
    • 90/90 External Rotation Bias
    • 90/90 Internal Rotation Bias
    • 90/90 external rotation strength
    • 90/90 internal rotation strength
      • 10-15 repetitions witha a 2-3 second hold
    • 90/90 transition
